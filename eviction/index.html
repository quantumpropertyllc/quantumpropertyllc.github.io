Mecklenburg Eviction Guide

Landlord's Guide to Eviction

This guide helps landlords in Mecklenburg County, NC understand the legal process of 'Summary Ejectment'. Follow the steps carefully to avoid legal issues.

Warning

Legal Warning

'Self-help' evictions (changing locks, cutting utilities) are illegal in NC. You must follow the court process.

Eviction Process

1

Give Proper Notice

You must provide a written notice before filing in court.

2

File Summary Ejectment

File form AOC-CVM-201 at the Mecklenburg County Courthouse.

3

Service of Process

The Sheriff must serve the summons and complaint to the tenant.

4

Attend Hearing

Hearings are usually 7-30 days after filing. Bring your lease.

5

Writ of Possession

Wait 10 days for appeal period, then file for a Writ of Possession.
